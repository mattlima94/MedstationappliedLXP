3. Asset-Light Expansion

Traditional healthcare expansion requires significant capital investment. Asset-light models enable faster, more efficient growth.

Traditional vs. Asset-Light Comparison

Factor Traditional Model Asset-Light Model
Capital Required $500K-$2M per location $100K-$300K per location
Time to Open 12-18 months 3-6 months
Facility Owned/built, large footprint Leased, right-sized space
Equipment Full diagnostic suite owned Essential equipment, outsource diagnostics
Staffing Large permanent staff Lean core team + flexible support
Risk High fixed costs, slow pivot Lower fixed costs, adaptable
Growth Speed 1-2 locations/year 3-5+ locations/year
üí° Key Concept

Medstation's Premium Service Center Model: Medstation uses an asset-light approach with standardized, efficient clinic designs, shared services (billing, IT, HR), and technology automation. This enables expansion from 7 to 10 locations while maintaining quality and culture.

5. Operational Metrics for Care Delivery

Successful healthcare operations require tracking key performance indicators across the care delivery process.

Key Operational Metrics

Metric Definition Target
Third Next Available Days until 3rd available appointment slot <3 days
No-Show Rate % of appointments where patient doesn't arrive <5%
Cycle Time Total time from check-in to checkout <45 minutes
Provider Utilization % of available slots filled 85-90%
Patient Satisfaction Survey scores (NPS, satisfaction) NPS >50
Care Gap Closure % of preventive care completed >80%
üí° Key Concept

Balancing Access and Efficiency: The best healthcare operations balance patient access (short wait times, easy scheduling) with provider efficiency (high utilization, appropriate visit mix). Technology like online scheduling, automated reminders, and telehealth options helps achieve both.
